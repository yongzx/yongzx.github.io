Yong Zheng-Xin

Computer Science Ph.D. student @ Brown University
Research Scientist Intern @ Meta AI (FAIR)

I am a rising fourth-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Brown University. I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Stephen Bach. My research interests are centered around multilingual, inclusive, and safe foundational models. I am a major contributor to open-source multilingual LLMs initiative, such as BigScience and Aya, as well as NLP development for Southeast-Asian languages (e.g., SEACrowd).

Currently, I am a research scientist intern at Meta AI (FAIR) researching multilingual/multimodal biases under the supervision of Jean Maillard. Prior to joining Ph.D., I obtained a B.Sc. in Computer Science from Minerva University, and I worked with linguists Prof. Tiago Torrent, Prof. Oliver Czulo, and Collin F. Baker on frame semantics during my undergrad.

I believe that intelligent systems should serve everyone around the world in a responsible way. To this end, I work on following research directions:

  1. making frontier AI safer for all language users – My discovery that low-resource languages can jailbreak GPT-4 pioneered and raised awareness for multilingual safety research (our work was featured in various media including New Scientist). Since then, I work on safety research for massively-multilingual foundational models.
    • I was part of the Responsible AI team for Aya (which supports 101 languages) at Cohere For AI.
    • I currently work on multimodal biases in Massively Multilingual Speech (MMS) models (which supports 1000+ languages) at Meta AI (FAIR).
  2. building open-source multilingual LMs – I’ve contributed to the earliest English and multilingual instruction-following LLMs, such as T0 (with PromptSource) and mT0/BLOOMZ. I was also involved in the development and evaluation of BLOOM model at BigScience, and I helped collect Malay instruction-following data (as language ambassador) and perform safety evaluation for Aya model.
  3. enabling AI to serve underrepresented populations – Frontier AI systems these days only cater to a small subset of language, leaving many communities across the world left unsupported. To support underrepresented languages, I led language adaption research at BigScience to efficiently adapt BLOOM to languages unseen during training. I also worked on developing NLP systems for South-East-Asian (SEA) languages by curating SEACrowd benchmark and showing how poorly multilingual LLMs code-switch in SEA languages.
